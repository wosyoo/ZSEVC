Enhancing Zero-Shot Emotional Voice Conversion via Speaker Adaptation and Duration Prediction

Shiyan Wang1,2, Tianhua Qi1,2, Cheng Lu1,2, Wenming Zheng1,2*
1 Key Laboratory of Child Development and Learning Science (Southeast University), Ministry of Education, Nanjing 210096, China
2 School of Biological Science and Medical Engineering, Southeast University, China

1. Abstract

Emotion Voice Conversion (EVC) aims to modify the emotional tone of speech while preserving the speaker's identity and the original content. Despite advancements in the field, existing EVC models often suffer from limited generalization abilities, and the acquisition of large-scale emotional datasets remains a labor-intensive and costly task. To address these challenges, we propose a Zero-Shot Emotion Voice Conversion (ZSEVC) model leveraging self-supervised learning. This model is designed to predict the duration of discrete speech units and adjust the speech rhythm to align with the target emotional state. A hierarchical emotion fusion module is incorporated to integrate emotional features with content features, thereby improving pronunciation and emotional expressiveness. Additionally, a residual speaker-emotion fusion module is introduced to enhance the adaptation of speaker characteristics to emotional expressions. Experimental results demonstrate that ZSEVC surpasses existing EVC models in terms of both naturalness and diversity of emotional expression. Moreover, it achieves high performance in zero-shot scenarios, successfully generating emotional speech for unseen emotions and speakers.
